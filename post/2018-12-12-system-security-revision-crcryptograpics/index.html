What is Cryptography?

In ancient Greek, kryptós means hidden or secret and, of course, graphy means to write. Generally, we consider cryptography as a practical study for secure communication, that is for preventing the third parties or the public from reading the private messages[1].

Basic Flow

1
2

plaintext--(key)-->encryption-->ciphertext-->(Communication)--
-->ciphertext--(key)-->decryption-->plaintext

Classical Cipher

  • Substitution Ciphers
    • Caesar
    • Monoaphabetic
    • Polyaphabetic
    • Playfair
    • Autokey
  • Transposition Ciphers
    • Rail Fence
    • Row Transposition

Stagenography

Stagenography aims to hide the existence of the information.

For example, we can use the invisible ink to write, hide a torrent link in a JPEG file (you know~) or write an artical where the information can be combined with every begining letters of each lines.

The disvantage of stagenoagraphy is that the overhead is huge even for a small piece of information.

Ciphers for a sequence of bits

Unlike the classical ciphers, modern ciphers deal with information in bits level.

  • Stream Ciphers
  • Block Ciphers
